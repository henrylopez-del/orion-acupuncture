Five rhythms inside one body.

In Traditional Chinese Medicine your body holds five rhythms. When one runs too loud or too quiet, the others bend around it. Sleep frays, cycles stutter, digestion slows, mood shifts. Every session begins by listening for which one is leading.

"Most of the diagnosis happens in the first ten minutes. Before any needle goes in, the body has already told you which rhythm is loudest."

Aoife · on channel palpation

Wood
№ 01 · Liver · Gallbladder · Spring

What it runs

Wood holds the smooth flow of qi through the body. The liver stores blood, regulates emotion, and keeps energy moving evenly. The gallbladder makes the decisions wood has to make. Tendons, ligaments, eyes, the sides of the head.

When it stalls

Irritability without a trigger. Tension in the shoulders and the sides of the neck. Periods that arrive with PMS and clots. Headaches at the temples. Frustration that feels disproportionate. Decisions that take days.

How we move it

  • Liver 3 between the first and second toes, the most-used point in the clinic
  • Scalp lines along the temples to release pressure
  • Cupping on the upper back when stress lives in the shoulders
  • A short herbal formula for cycle support, two weeks at a time

"Wood is the first to seize when life gets noisy. Branches in winter. The work is to coax them back into spring."

Wood, in plain words

Fire
№ 02 · Heart · Small Intestine · Summer

What it runs

Fire holds the mind, what Chinese medicine calls shen. The heart governs awareness, joy, and the quality of sleep. The small intestine sorts what is worth keeping from what is not, at the level of food and at the level of thought.

When it stalls

Waking at three in the morning with a busy mind. Anxiety that flares without a cause. Heart palpitations on a quiet afternoon. Tongue tip very red. Speech that runs ahead of the room.

How we move it

  • Heart 7 at the wrist, the calming pulse point
  • Pericardium 6 for chest tension and racing thoughts
  • Yin Tang between the brows when sleep is the symptom
  • Warm foot soaks with mugwort, fifteen minutes before bed
Earth
№ 03 · Spleen · Stomach · Late summer

What it runs

Earth is the engine. The spleen transforms food and fluids into the qi and blood the rest of the body lives on. The stomach receives. Muscles, the centre of the body, the way you actually digest the day.

When it stalls

Bloating after lunch. The 3pm crash with no obvious cause. Brain fog. Loose stools that come and go. Sweet cravings late in the afternoon. The feeling that no food gives you what it should.

How we move it

  • Stomach 36 below the knee, a point that builds you back up over weeks
  • Spleen 6 on the inside of the calf for energy and cycle steadiness
  • Direct moxa over the navel when digestion runs cold
  • A list of foods that actually rebuild qi — short, cooked, warming
Metal
№ 04 · Lung · Large Intestine · Autumn

What it runs

Metal holds the boundary between inside and out. The lung governs breath, skin, and the immune barrier we call wei qi. The large intestine releases what no longer serves. Grief lives here, and so does the capacity to let it pass through.

When it stalls

Eczema that flares with stress. A dry cough that lingers after a cold. Recurring chest infections in the Dublin winter. A nose that runs at the start of every season. Grief that hasn't finished moving.

How we move it

  • Lung 7 at the wrist, for cough and grief together
  • Large Intestine 4 in the web of the hand, to release the surface
  • Gua sha across the upper back when wind has lodged there
  • Warm congee with white foods through the season — pear, lily bulb, almond
Water
№ 05 · Kidney · Bladder · Winter

What it runs

Water is the deep tank. The kidneys hold essence, hormones, bones, fertility, the will to keep going. The bladder receives. Fear lives here in a primal sense, and so does the steadiness that lets you face what you actually have to.

When it stalls

Burnout that doesn't lift with one weekend off. Lower-back ache that comes with stress. Cycles that lengthen or skip. Tinnitus. Premature greying. The feeling that you are running on a credit line your body has not approved.

How we move it

  • Kidney 3 at the inner ankle, the point that refills the tank slowly
  • Bladder 23 on the lower back for adrenal fatigue
  • Ren 4 below the navel for fertility protocols and post-illness recovery
  • Earlier nights for two weeks, the most underrated prescription in the clinic
