@acuwithgigi/First session

Ninety minutes to read the body.

The first session is longer than the rest. We need time to actually listen, take both pulses, look at the tongue, find the pattern. Then we treat. You go home with a printed protocol for the week.

i.

Intake

We talk about sleep, appetite, cycle, mood, how often you sigh. Then we read your pulse on both wrists and look at your tongue. No paperwork shortcuts.

ii.

Treatment

Forty-five minutes on a heated table. Twelve to twenty very fine needles, sometimes moxa, sometimes cupping. You'll feel a few of them. Most patients fall asleep.

iii.

Aftercare

A printed protocol: foods, herbs, simple rituals for the week. We don't recommend supplements you can't pronounce. We do recommend a hot foot soak before bed.

Notes for before you come.

Eat something an hour before

Acupuncture on an empty stomach can make a sensitive system light-headed. Something small and warm an hour before the session works well. A bowl of porridge, a piece of toast, a tea.

Wear loose layers

We treat points on the calves, forearms, lower back, and belly. Loose trousers that roll up easily and a soft top make the session smoother. The room is warm and there's a heated table.

What the needles feel like

About the width of a hair. You'll feel a few of them as a small heaviness or a brief tingle. Most are inserted without sensation. The needles stay in for twenty to thirty minutes while you rest.

Afterwards

Most patients leave loose and a little dreamy. Some sleep deeper that night. A small number feel a brief tiredness for a few hours, which is normal as the system resets. Drink water, eat warm food, plan a quiet evening.

How often we treat

Acute conditions: weekly for three to six sessions, then we review. Chronic conditions like cycle support, fertility, or burnout: weekly for the first month, then fortnightly. Maintenance: every four to six weeks, alongside the season changes.

Cancellation

The studio holds one patient at a time. Twenty-four hours' notice for a cancellation, please. Less than that, we charge half. We understand life happens, and we're flexible the first time it does.
